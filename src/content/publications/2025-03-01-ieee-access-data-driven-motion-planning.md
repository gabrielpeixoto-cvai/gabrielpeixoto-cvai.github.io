## Abstract

Recent advances in deep neural networks, reinforcement learning, and large language models have opened new possibilities for solving motion planning problems. This survey reviews data-driven motion planning across these three paradigms, examining how they improve sampling efficiency, optimize control policies, and enable task planning from natural-language instructions, and it discusses the open challenges and future directions for the field.
